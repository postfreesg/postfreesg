What is ABSD in Singapore?

ABSD, also known as Additional Buyerâ€™s Stamp Duty, is a tax payable on top of Buyerâ€™s Stamp Duty when purchasing residential property in Singapore. It was introduced to ensure a stable and sustainable property market by discouraging excessive investment demand.

ABSD applies based on:

  • Buyer profile (SC, SPR, foreigner, entity)
  • Number of residential properties owned
  • Type of buyer (individual or company)

Why ABSD Matters

Understanding ABSD helps property buyers estimate the true upfront cost of purchasing a home or investment unit. ABSD can significantly increase the overall purchase amount, especially for second and third properties.

Singapore ABSD Rates

The ABSD rate depends on your residency status and how many properties you already own. Here is a simplified view of the structure:

  • Singapore Citizens: 0% for first property, higher for additional properties
  • Singapore PRs: ABSD applies from first property onward
  • Foreigners: Flat higher rate regardless of property count
  • Entities and Developers: Highest ABSD tier applies

How ABSD Affects Property Buyers

ABSD can influence your affordability, investment return, and long-term planning. Buyers often use ABSD calculations to compare:

  • Upgrading vs buying a second property
  • Decoupling strategies
  • Investment vs own-stay decisions
  • Cost differences between new launch and resale units

For investors, ABSD can significantly affect net rental yield and the breakeven timeline.

How to Reduce or Avoid ABSD

Certain legal methods may reduce ABSD exposure, depending on eligibility:

  • Buying under a single name instead of joint names
  • Decoupling (subject to eligibility and costs)
  • Buying a commercial property with no ABSD
  • Right-sizing before buying another residential unit
